# Article 6 - Control framework requirements

## 1.
*[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|Administrators]]* shall have in place a control framework that ensures that their *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmarks]]* are provided and published or made available in accordance with this Regulation.
## 2.
The control framework shall be proportionate to the level of conflicts of interest identified, the extent of discretion in the provision of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* and the nature of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(14)|input data]]*.
## 3.
The control framework shall include:
### (a)
management of operational risk;
### (b)
adequate and effective business continuity and disaster recovery plans;
### (c)
contingency procedures that are in place in the event of a disruption to the process of the provision of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]*.
## 4.
An *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* shall establish measures to:
### (a)
ensure that *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(9)|contributors]]* adhere to the code of conduct referred to in [[2016R1011_EN.15|Article 15]] and comply with the applicable standards for *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(14)|input data]]*;
### (b)
monitor *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(14)|input data]]* including, where feasible, monitoring *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(14)|input data]]* before publication of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* and validating *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(14)|input data]]* after publication to identify errors and anomalies.
## 5.
The control framework shall be documented, reviewed and updated as appropriate and made available to the relevant competent authority and, upon request, to users.
## 6.
For *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(25)|critical benchmarks]]*, an *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* shall have sound administrative and accounting procedures, internal control mechanisms, effective procedures for risk assessment, and effective control and safeguard arrangements for managing ICT systems in accordance with Regulation (EU) 2022/2554 of the European Parliament and of the Council.
