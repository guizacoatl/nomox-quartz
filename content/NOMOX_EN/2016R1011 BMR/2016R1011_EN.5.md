# Article 5 - Oversight function requirements

## 1.
*[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|Administrators]]* shall establish and maintain a permanent and effective oversight function to ensure oversight of all aspects of the provision of their *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmarks]]*.
## 2.
*[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|Administrators]]* shall develop and maintain robust procedures regarding their oversight function, which shall be made available to the relevant competent authorities.
## 3.
The oversight function shall operate with integrity and shall have the following responsibilities, which shall be adjusted by the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* based on the complexity, use and vulnerability of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]*:
### (a)
reviewing the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]*'s definition and methodology at least annually;
### (b)
overseeing any changes to the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* methodology and being able to request the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* to consult on such changes;
### (c)
overseeing the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]*'s control framework, the management and operation of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]*, and, where the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* is based on *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(14)|input data]]* from *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(9)|contributors]]*, the code of conduct referred to in [[2016R1011_EN.15|Article 15]];
### (d)
reviewing and approving procedures for cessation of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]*, including any consultation about a cessation;
### (e)
overseeing any third party involved in the provision of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]*, including calculation or dissemination agents;
### (f)
assessing internal and external audits or reviews, and monitoring the implementation of identified remedial actions;
### (g)
where the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* is based on *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(14)|input data]]* from *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(9)|contributors]]*, monitoring the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(14)|input data]]* and *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(9)|contributors]]* and the actions of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* in challenging or validating contributions of *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(14)|input data]]*;
### (h)
where the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* is based on *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(14)|input data]]* from *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(9)|contributors]]*, taking effective measures in respect of any breaches of the code of conduct referred to in [[2016R1011_EN.15|Article 15]]; and
#### (i)
reporting to the relevant competent authorities any misconduct by *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(9)|contributors]]*, where the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* is based on *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(14)|input data]]* from *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(9)|contributors]]*, or *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrators]]*, of which the oversight function becomes aware, and any anomalous or suspicious *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(14)|input data]]*.
## 4.
The oversight function shall be carried out by a separate committee or by means of another appropriate governance arrangement.
## 5.
ESMA shall develop draft regulatory technical standards to specify the procedures regarding the oversight function and the characteristics of the oversight function including its composition as well as its positioning within the organisational structure of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]*, so as to ensure the integrity of the function and the absence of conflicts of interest. In particular, ESMA shall develop a non-exhaustive list of appropriate governance arrangements as laid down in paragraph 4.

ESMA shall distinguish between the different types of *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmarks]]* and sectors as set out in this Regulation and shall take into consideration the differences in the ownership and control structure of *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrators]]*, the nature, scale and complexity of the provision of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]*, and the risk and impact of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]*, also in light of international convergence of supervisory practice in relation to governance requirements of *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmarks]]*. However, the ESMA draft regulatory technical standards shall not cover or apply to *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrators]]* of *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(27)|non-significant benchmarks]]*.

ESMA shall submit those draft regulatory technical standards to the Commission by 1 April 2017.

Power is delegated to the Commission to adopt the regulatory technical standards referred to in the first subparagraph in accordance with the procedure laid down in Articles [[2010R1095_EN.10|10]] to [[2010R1095_EN.14|14]] of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010.
## 6.
ESMA may issue guidelines in accordance with [[2010R1095_EN.16|Article 16 of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010]], addressed to *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrators]]* of *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(27)|non-significant benchmarks]]* to specify the elements referred to in paragraph 5 of this Article.
