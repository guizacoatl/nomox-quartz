# Article 33 - Endorsement of benchmarks provided in a third country

## 1.
An *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(28)|located]]* in the Union and authorised or registered in accordance with [[2016R1011_EN.34|Article 34]], or any other *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(17)|supervised entity]]* *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(28)|located]]* in the Union with a clear and well-defined role within the control or accountability framework of a third country *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]*, which is able to monitor effectively the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(5)|provision of a benchmark]]*, may apply to the relevant competent authority to endorse a *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* or a *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(4)|family of benchmarks]]* provided in a third country for their use in the Union, provided that all of the following conditions are fulfilled:
### (a)
the endorsing *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* or other *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(17)|supervised entity]]* has verified and is able to demonstrate on an on-going basis to its competent authority that the provision of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* or *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(4)|family of benchmarks]]* to be endorsed fulfils, on a mandatory or on a voluntary basis, requirements which are at least as stringent as the requirements of this Regulation;
### (b)
the endorsing *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* or other *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(17)|supervised entity]]* has the necessary expertise to monitor effectively the activity of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(5)|provision of a benchmark]]* in a third country and to manage the associated risks;
### (c)
there is an objective reason to provide the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* or *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(4)|family of benchmarks]]* in a third country and for said *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* or *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(4)|family of benchmarks]]* to be endorsed for their use in the Union.

For the purpose of point (a), when assessing whether the provision of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* or *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(4)|family of benchmarks]]* to be endorsed fulfils requirements which are at least as stringent as the requirements of this Regulation, the competent authority may take into account whether the compliance of the provision of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* or *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(4)|family of benchmarks]]* with the IOSCO principles for financial *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmarks]]* or the IOSCO principles for PRAs, as applicable, would be equivalent to compliance with the requirements of this Regulation.
## 2.
An *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* or other *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(17)|supervised entity]]* that makes an application for endorsement as referred to in paragraph 1 shall provide all information necessary to satisfy the competent authority that, at the time of application, all the conditions referred to in that paragraph are fulfilled.
## 3.
Within 90 working days of receipt of the application for endorsement referred to in paragraph 1, the relevant competent authority shall examine the application and adopt a decision either to authorise the endorsement or to refuse it. An endorsed *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* or an endorsed *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(4)|family of benchmarks]]* shall be notified by the competent authority to ESMA.
## 4.
An endorsed *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* or an endorsed *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(4)|family of benchmarks]]* shall be considered to be a *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* or *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(4)|family of benchmarks]]* provided by the endorsing *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* or other *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(17)|supervised entity]]*. The endorsing *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* or other *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(17)|supervised entity]]* shall not use the endorsement with the intention of avoiding the requirements of this Regulation.
## 5.
An *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* or other *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(17)|supervised entity]]* that has endorsed a *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* or a *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(4)|family of benchmarks]]* provided in a third country shall remain fully responsible for such a *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* or *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(4)|family of benchmarks]]* and for compliance with the obligations under this Regulation.
## 6.
Where the competent authority of the endorsing *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* or other *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(17)|supervised entity]]* has well-founded reasons to consider that the conditions laid down under paragraph 1 of this Article are no longer fulfilled, it shall have the power to require the endorsing *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* or other *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(17)|supervised entity]]* to cease the endorsement and shall inform ESMA thereof. [[2016R1011_EN.28|Article 28]] shall apply in case of cessation of the endorsement.
## 7.
The Commission shall be empowered to adopt delegated acts in accordance with [[2016R1011_EN.49|Article 49]] concerning measures to determine the conditions under which the relevant competent authorities may assess whether there is an objective reason for the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(5)|provision of a benchmark]]* or *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(4)|family of benchmarks]]* in a third country and their endorsement for their use in the Union. The Commission shall take into account elements such as the specificities of the underlying market or economic reality the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* intends to measure, the need for proximity of the provision of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* to such market or economic reality, the need for proximity of the provision of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* to *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(9)|contributors]]*, the material availability of *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(14)|input data]]* due to different time zones, and specific skills required in the provision of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]*.
