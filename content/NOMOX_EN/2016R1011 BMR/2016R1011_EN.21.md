> Level 2 reference(s): [[2018R1649_EN.0]]# Article 21 - Mandatory administration of a critical benchmark

## 1.
If an *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* of a *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(25)|critical benchmark]]* intends to cease providing such *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]*, the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* shall:
### (a)
immediately notify its competent authority; and
### (b)
within four weeks of such notification submit an assessment of how the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]*:
#### (i)
is to be transitioned to a new *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]*; or
#### (ii)
is to be ceased to be provided, taking into account the procedure established in [[2016R1011_EN.28#1|Article 28(1)]].

During the period referred to in point (b) of the first subparagraph, the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* shall not cease provision of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]*.
## 2.
Upon receipt of the assessment by the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* referred to in paragraph 1, the competent authority shall:
### (a)
inform ESMA and the college established under [[2016R1011_EN.46|Article 46]];
### (b)
within four weeks following the receipt of that assessment, make its own assessment of how the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* is to be transitioned to a new *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* or be ceased to be provided, taking into account the procedure established in accordance with [[2016R1011_EN.28#1|Article 28(1)]].

During the period referred to in point (b) of the first subparagraph, the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* shall not cease the provision of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* without the written consent of ESMA or the competent authority, where relevant.
## 3.
Following completion of the assessment referred to in point (b) of paragraph 2, the competent authority shall have the power to compel the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* to continue publishing the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* until such time as:
### (a)
the provision of the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* has been transitioned to a new *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]*;
### (b)
the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* can be ceased to be provided in an orderly fashion; or
### (c)
the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* is no longer critical.

For the purposes of the first subparagraph, the period for which the competent authority may compel the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* to continue to publish the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]* shall not exceed 12 months.

By the end of that period, the competent authority shall review its decision to compel the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* to continue to publish the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(3)|benchmark]]*. The competent authority may, where necessary, extend that period by an appropriate period not exceeding 12 months. The maximum period of mandatory administration shall not exceed five years.
## 4.
Without prejudice to paragraph 1, in the event that the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* of a *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(25)|critical benchmark]]* is to be wound down due to insolvency proceedings, the competent authority shall make an assessment of whether and how the *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(25)|critical benchmark]]* can be transitioned to a new *[[2016R1011_EN.3#(6)|administrator]]* or can cease to be provided in an orderly fashion, taking into account the procedure established in accordance with [[2016R1011_EN.28#1|Article 28(1)]].
## 5.
ESMA shall develop draft regulatory technical standards to specify the criteria on which the assessment referred to in point (b) of paragraph 2 is to be based.

ESMA shall submit those draft regulatory technical standards to the Commission by 1 October 2020.

Power is delegated to the Commission to supplement this Regulation by adopting the regulatory technical standards referred to in the first subparagraph in accordance with Articles [[2010R1095_EN.10|10]] to [[2010R1095_EN.14|14]] of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010.
