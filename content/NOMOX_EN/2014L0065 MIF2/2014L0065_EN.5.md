# Article 5 - Requirement for authorisation

## 1.
Each Member State shall require that the provision of investment services and/or the performance of investment activities as a regular occupation or business on a professional basis be subject to prior authorisation in accordance with this Chapter. Such authorisation shall be granted by the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(55)|home Member State]]* *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(26)|competent authority]]* designated in accordance with [[2014L0065_EN.67|Article 67]].
## 2.
By way of derogation from paragraph 1, Member States shall authorise any *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(18)|market operator]]* to operate an *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(22)|MTF]]* or an *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(23)|OTF]]*, subject to the prior verification of their compliance with this Chapter.
## 3.
Member States shall register all *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firms]]*. The register shall be publicly accessible and shall contain information on the services or activities for which the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* is authorised. It shall be updated on a regular basis. Every authorisation shall be notified to ESMA.

ESMA shall establish a list of all *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firms]]* in the Union. That list shall contain information on the services or activities for which each *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* is authorised and it shall be updated on a regular basis. ESMA shall publish and keep up-to-date that list on its website.

Where a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(26)|competent authority]]* has withdrawn an authorisation in accordance with points (b), (c) and (d) of [[2014L0065_EN.8|Article 8]], that withdrawal shall be published on the list for a period of five years.
## 4.
Each Member State shall require that:
### (a)
any *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* which is a legal person have its head office in the same Member State as its registered office;
### (b)
any *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* which is not a legal person or any *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* which is a legal person but under its national law has no registered office, have its head office in the Member State in which it actually carries out its business.
