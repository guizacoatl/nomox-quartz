# Article 25 - Assessment of suitability and appropriateness and reporting to clients

## 1.
Member States shall require *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firms]]* to ensure and demonstrate to competent authorities on request that natural persons giving *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(4)|investment advice]]* or information about *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(15)|financial instruments]]*, investment services or *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(3)|ancillary services]]* to *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|clients]]* on behalf of the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* possess the necessary knowledge and competence to fulfil their obligations under [[2014L0065_EN.24|Article 24]] and this Article. Member States shall publish the criteria to be used for assessing such knowledge and competence.
## 2.
When providing *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(4)|investment advice]]* or *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(8)|portfolio management]]* the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* shall obtain the necessary information regarding the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]*’s or potential *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]*’s knowledge and experience in the investment field relevant to the specific type of product or service, that person’s financial situation including his ability to bear losses, and his investment objectives including his risk tolerance so as to enable the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* to recommend to the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* or potential *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* the investment services and *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(15)|financial instruments]]* that are suitable for him and, in particular, are in accordance with his risk tolerance and ability to bear losses.

Member States shall ensure that where an *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* provides *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(4)|investment advice]]* recommending a package of services or products bundled pursuant to [[2014L0065_EN.24#11|Article 24(11)]], the overall bundled package is suitable.

When providing either *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(4)|investment advice]]* or *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(8)|portfolio management]]* that involves the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(8a)|switching of financial instruments]]*, *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firms]]* shall obtain the necessary information on the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]*’s investment and shall analyse the costs and benefits of the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(8a)|switching of financial instruments]]*. When providing *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(4)|investment advice]]*, *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firms]]* shall inform the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* whether or not the benefits of the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(8a)|switching of financial instruments]]* are greater than the costs involved in such switching.
## 3.
Member States shall ensure that *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firms]]*, when providing investment services other than those referred to in paragraph 2, ask the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* or potential *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* to provide information regarding that person’s knowledge and experience in the investment field relevant to the specific type of product or service offered or demanded so as to enable the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* to assess whether the investment service or product envisaged is appropriate for the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]*. Where a bundle of services or products is envisaged pursuant to [[2014L0065_EN.24#11|Article 24(11)]], the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#2.|assessment]]* shall consider whether the overall bundled package is appropriate.

Where the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* considers, on the basis of the information received under the first subparagraph, that the product or service is not appropriate to the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* or potential *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]*, the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* shall warn the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* or potential *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]*. That warning may be provided in a standardised format.

Where *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|clients]]* or potential *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|clients]]* do not provide the information referred to under the first subparagraph, or where they provide insufficient information regarding their knowledge and experience, the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* shall warn them that the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* is not in a position to determine whether the service or product envisaged is appropriate for them. That warning may be provided in a standardised format.
## 4.
Member States shall allow *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firms]]* when providing investment services that only consist of execution or reception and transmission of *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* orders with or without *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(3)|ancillary services]]*, excluding the granting of credits or loans as specified in Section B.1 of Annex I that do not comprise of existing credit limits of loans, current accounts and overdraft facilities of *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|clients]]*, to provide those investment services to their *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|clients]]* without the need to obtain the information or make the determination provided for in paragraph 3 where all the following conditions are met:
### (a)
the services relate to any of the following *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(15)|financial instruments]]*:
#### (i)
shares admitted to trading on a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(21)|regulated market]]* or on an equivalent third-country market or on a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(22)|MTF]]*, where those are shares in companies, and excluding shares in non-UCITS collective investment undertakings and shares that embed a derivative;
#### (ii)
bonds or other forms of securitised debt admitted to trading on a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(21)|regulated market]]* or on an equivalent third country market or on a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(22)|MTF]]*, excluding those that embed a derivative or incorporate a structure which makes it difficult for the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* to understand the risk involved;
#### (iii)
*[[2014L0065_EN.4#(17)|money-market instruments]]*, excluding those that embed a derivative or incorporate a structure which makes it difficult for the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* to understand the risk involved;
#### (iv)
shares or units in UCITS, excluding structured UCITS as referred to in the second subparagraph of [[2010R0583_EN.36#1|Article 36(1) of Regulation (EU) No 583/2010]];
#### (v)
*[[2014L0065_EN.4#(43)|structured deposits]]*, excluding those that incorporate a structure which makes it difficult for the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* to understand the risk of return or the cost of exiting the product before term;
#### (vi)
other non-complex *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(15)|financial instruments]]* for the purpose of this paragraph.

For the purpose of this point, a third-country market shall be considered to be equivalent to a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(21)|regulated market]]* if the requirements and the procedure laid down under the third and the fourth subparagraphs are fulfilled.

At the request of the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(26)|competent authority]]* of a Member State, the Commission shall adopt equivalence decisions in accordance with the examination procedure referred to in [[2014L0065_EN.89a#2|Article 89a(2)]], stating whether the legal and supervisory framework of a third country ensures that a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(21)|regulated market]]* authorised in that third country complies with legally binding requirements which are, for the purpose of the application of this point, equivalent to the requirements resulting from Regulation (EU) No 596/2014, from Title III of this Directive, from Title II of Regulation (EU) No 600/2014 and from Directive 2004/109/EC, and which are subject to effective supervision and enforcement in that third country. The *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(26)|competent authority]]* shall indicate why it considers that the legal and supervisory framework of the third country concerned is to be considered equivalent and shall provide relevant information to that end.

Such third-country legal and supervisory framework may be considered equivalent where that framework fulfils at least the following conditions:
#### (i)
the markets are subject to authorisation and to effective supervision and enforcement on an ongoing basis;
#### (ii)
the markets have clear and transparent rules regarding the admission of securities to trading so that such securities are capable of being traded in a fair, orderly and efficient manner, and are freely negotiable;
#### (iii)
security issuers are subject to periodic and ongoing information requirements ensuring a high level of investor protection; and
#### (iv)
market transparency and integrity are ensured by the prevention of market abuse in the form of insider dealing and market manipulation.
### (b)
the service is provided at the initiative of the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* or potential *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]*;
### (c)
the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* or potential *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* has been clearly informed that in the provision of that service the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* is not required to assess the appropriateness of the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(15)|financial instrument]]* or service provided or offered and that therefore he does not benefit from the corresponding protection of the relevant conduct of business rules. Such a warning may be provided in a standardised format;
### (d)
the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* complies with its obligations under [[2014L0065_EN.23|Article 23]].
## 5.
The *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* shall establish a record that includes the document or documents agreed between the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* and the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* that set out the rights and obligations of the parties, and the other terms on which the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* will provide services to the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]*. The rights and duties of the parties to the contract may be incorporated by reference to other documents or legal texts.
## 6.
The *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* shall provide the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* with adequate reports on the service provided in a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(62)|durable medium]]*. Those reports shall include periodic communications to *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|clients]]*, taking into account the type and the complexity of *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(15)|financial instruments]]* involved and the nature of the service provided to the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* and shall include, where applicable, the costs associated with the transactions and services undertaken on behalf of the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]*.

When providing *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(4)|investment advice]]*, the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* shall, before the transaction is made, provide the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* with a statement on suitability in a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(62)|durable medium]]* specifying the advice given and how that advice meets the preferences, objectives and other characteristics of the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(11)|retail client]]*.

Where the agreement to buy or sell a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(15)|financial instrument]]* is concluded using a means of distance communication which prevents the prior delivery of the suitability statement, the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* may provide the written statement on suitability in a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(62)|durable medium]]* immediately after the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* is bound by any agreement, provided both the following conditions are met:
### (a)
the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* has consented to receiving the suitability statement without undue delay after the conclusion of the transaction; and
### (b)
the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* has given the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* the option of delaying the transaction in order to receive the statement on suitability in advance.

Where an *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* provides *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(8)|portfolio management]]* or has informed the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* that it will carry out a periodic *[[2014L0065_EN.4#2.|assessment]]* of suitability, the periodic report shall contain an updated statement of how the investment meets the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]*’s preferences, objectives and other characteristics of the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(11)|retail client]]*.
## 7.
If a credit agreement relating to residential immovable property, which is subject to the provisions concerning creditworthiness *[[2014L0065_EN.4#2.|assessment]]* of consumers laid down in Directive 2014/17/EU of the European Parliament and the Council, has as a prerequisite the provision to that same consumer of an investment service in relation to mortgage bonds specifically issued to secure the financing of and having identical terms as the credit agreement relating to residential immovable property, in order for the loan to be payable, refinanced or redeemed, that service shall not be subject to the obligations set out in this Article.
## 8.
The Commission shall be empowered to adopt delegated acts in accordance with [[2014L0065_EN.89|Article 89]] to ensure that *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firms]]* comply with the principles set out in paragraphs 2 to 6 of this Article when providing investment or *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(3)|ancillary services]]* to their *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|clients]]*, including information to obtain when assessing the suitability or appropriateness of the services and *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(15)|financial instruments]]* for their *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|clients]]*, criteria to assess non-complex *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(15)|financial instruments]]* for the purposes of point (a)(vi) of paragraph 4 of this Article, the content and the format of records and agreements for the provision of services to *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|clients]]* and of periodic reports to *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|clients]]* on the services provided. Those delegated acts shall take into account:
### (a)
the nature of the service(s) offered or provided to the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* or potential *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]*, having regard to the type, object, size and frequency of the transactions;
### (b)
the nature of the products being offered or considered, including different types of *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(15)|financial instruments]]*;
### (c)
the retail or professional nature of the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* or potential *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|clients]]* or, in the case of paragraph 6, their classification as eligible counterparties.
## 9.
ESMA shall adopt by 3 January 2016 guidelines specifying criteria for the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#2.|assessment]]* of knowledge and competence required under paragraph 1.
## 10.
ESMA shall develop by 3 January 2016, and update periodically, guidelines for the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#2.|assessment]]* of:
### (a)
*[[2014L0065_EN.4#(15)|financial instruments]]* incorporating a structure which makes it difficult for the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* to understand the risk involved in accordance with points (a)(ii) and (a)(iii) of paragraph 4;
### (b)
*[[2014L0065_EN.4#(43)|structured deposits]]* incorporating a structure which makes it difficult for the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* to understand the risk of return or the cost of exiting the product before term, in accordance with point (a)(v) of paragraph 4.
## 11.
ESMA may develop guidelines, and update them periodically, for the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#2.|assessment]]* of *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(15)|financial instruments]]* being classified as non-complex for the purpose of point (a)(vi) of paragraph 4, taking into account the delegated acts adopted under paragraph 8.
