# Article 28 - Client order handling rules

## 1.
Member States shall require that *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firms]]* authorised to execute orders on behalf of *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|clients]]* implement procedures and arrangements which provide for the prompt, fair and expeditious execution of *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* orders, relative to other *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* orders or the trading interests of the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]*.

Those procedures or arrangements shall allow for the execution of otherwise comparable *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* orders in accordance with the time of their reception by the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]*.
## 2.
Member States shall require that, in the case of a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(14)|limit order]]* in respect of shares admitted to trading on a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(21)|regulated market]]* or traded on a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(24)|trading venue]]* which are not immediately executed under prevailing market conditions, *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firms]]* are, unless the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* expressly instructs otherwise, to take measures to facilitate the earliest possible execution of that order by making public immediately that *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(14)|limit order]]* in a manner which is easily accessible to other market participants. Member States may decide that *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firms]]* comply with that obligation by transmitting the *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(14)|limit order]]* to a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(24)|trading venue]]*. Member States shall provide that the competent authorities may waive the obligation to make public a *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(14)|limit order]]* that is large in scale compared with normal market size as determined under [[2014R0600_EN.4|Article 4 of Regulation (EU) No 600/2014]].
## 3.
The Commission shall be empowered to adopt delegated acts in accordance with [[2014L0065_EN.89|Article 89]] to define:
### (a)
the conditions and nature of the procedures and arrangements which result in the prompt, fair and expeditious execution of *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* orders and the situations in which or types of transaction for which *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firms]]* may reasonably deviate from prompt execution so as to obtain more favourable terms for *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|clients]]*;
### (b)
the different methods through which an *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(1)|investment firm]]* can be deemed to have met its obligation to disclose not immediately executable *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(9)|client]]* *[[2014L0065_EN.4#(14)|limit orders]]* to the market.
