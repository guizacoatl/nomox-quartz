# Article 46 - Review


By 1 January 2022, the Commission shall present a report to the European Parliament and the Council on the functioning of this Regulation, accompanied, if appropriate, by a legislative proposal.

That report shall consider in particular the findings of the reports referred to in [[2017R2402_EN.44|Article 44]], and shall assess:
### (a)
the effects of this Regulation, including the introduction of the *[[2017R2402_EN.2#1.|STS]]* *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(1)|securitisation]]* designation, on the functioning of the market for *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(1)|securitisations]]* in the Union, the contribution of *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(1)|securitisation]]* to the real economy, in particular on access to credit for SMEs and investments, and interconnectedness between financial institutions and the stability of the financial sector;
### (b)
the differences in use of the modalities referred to in [[2017R2402_EN.6#3|Article 6(3)]], based on the data reported pursuant to point (e)(iii) of the first subparagraph of [[2017R2402_EN.7#1|Article 7(1)]]. If the findings show an increase in prudential risks caused by the use of the modalities referred to in points (a), (b), (c) and (e) of [[2017R2402_EN.6#3|Article 6(3)]], then suitable redress shall be considered;
### (c)
whether there has been a disproportionate rise of the number of transactions referred to in the third subparagraph of [[2017R2402_EN.7#2|Article 7(2)]], since the application of this Regulation and whether market participants structured transactions in a way to circumvent the obligation under [[2017R2402_EN.7|Article 7]] to make available information through *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(1)|securitisation]]* repositories;
### (d)
whether there is a need to extend disclosure requirements under [[2017R2402_EN.7|Article 7]] to cover transactions referred to in the third subparagraph of [[2017R2402_EN.7#2|Article 7(2)]] and *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(11)|investor]]* positions;
### (e)
whether in the area of *[[2017R2402_EN.2#1.|STS]]* *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(1)|securitisations]]* an equivalence regime could be introduced for third-country *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(3)|originators]]*, *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(5)|sponsors]]* and *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(2)|SSPEs]]*, taking into consideration international developments in the area of *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(1)|securitisation]]*, in particular initiatives on simple, transparent and comparable *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(1)|securitisations]]*;
### (f)
the implementation of the requirements set out in Articles [[2017R2402_EN.22|22]](4) and 26d(4) and whether they may be extended to *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(1)|securitisation]]* where the underlying exposures are not residential loans or auto loans or leases, with a view to mainstreaming environmental, social and governance disclosure;
### (g)
the appropriateness of the third-party verification regime as provided for in Articles [[2017R2402_EN.27|27]] and [[2017R2402_EN.28|28]], and whether the authorisation regime for third parties provided for in [[2017R2402_EN.28|Article 28]] fosters sufficient competition among third parties and whether changes in the supervisory framework need to be introduced in order to ensure financial stability; and
### (h)
whether there is a need to complement the framework on *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(1)|securitisation]]* set out in this Regulation by establishing a system of limited licensed banks, performing the functions of *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(2)|SSPEs]]* and having the exclusive right to purchase exposures from *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(3)|originators]]* and sell claims backed by the purchased exposures to *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(11)|investors]]*; and
#### (i)
the possibility for further standardisation and disclosure requirements in view of evolving market practices, namely through the use of templates, for both traditional and *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(10)|synthetic securitisations]]*, including for bespoke private *[[2017R2402_EN.2#(1)|securitisations]]* where no prospectus has to be drawn up in compliance with Regulation (EU) 2017/1129 of the European Parliament and of the Council.
