# Article 22 - Conditions for resolution

## 1.
The *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(3)|resolution authority]]* shall take a *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(11)|resolution action]]* in relation to a *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* provided that all of the following conditions are met:
### (a)
the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* is failing or is likely to fail as determined by any of the following:
#### (i)
the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(7)|competent authority]]*, after consulting the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(3)|resolution authority]]*;
#### (ii)
the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(3)|resolution authority]]* after consulting the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(7)|competent authority]]*, where the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(3)|resolution authority]]* has the necessary tools for reaching that conclusion;
### (b)
there is no reasonable prospect that any alternative private sector measures, including the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]*’s *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(22)|recovery plan]]* or other contractual arrangements, or supervisory action, including early intervention measures taken, would prevent the failure of the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* within a reasonable timeframe, having regard to all relevant circumstances;
### (c)
a *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(11)|resolution action]]* is necessary in the public interest to achieve, while being proportionate to, one or more of the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(6)|resolution objectives]]*, and winding up the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* under normal insolvency procedures would not meet those *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(6)|resolution objectives]]* to the same extent.
## 2.
For the purposes of point (a)(ii) of paragraph 1, the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(7)|competent authority]]* shall provide the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(3)|resolution authority]]* on its own initiative and without delay with any information that may give an indication that the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* is failing or likely to fail. The *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(7)|competent authority]]* shall also provide the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(3)|resolution authority]]* upon request with any other information needed in order to perform its assessment.
## 3.
For the purposes of point (a) of paragraph 1, a *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* shall be deemed to be failing or likely to fail where one or more of the following circumstances apply:
### (a)
the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* infringes, or is likely to infringe, its authorisation requirements in a way that would justify the withdrawal of its authorisation pursuant to [[2012R0648_EN.20|Article 20 of Regulation (EU) No 648/2012]];
### (b)
the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* is unable, or is likely to be unable, to provide a critical function;
### (c)
the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* is unable, or is likely to be unable, to restore its viability through the implementation of its recovery measures;
### (d)
the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* is unable, or is likely to be unable, to pay its debts or other liabilities as they fall due;
### (e)
the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* requires *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(30)|extraordinary public financial support]]*.
## 4.
For the purposes of point (e) of paragraph 3, public financial support shall not be considered *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(30)|extraordinary public financial support]]* where it meets all of the following conditions:
### (a)
it takes the form of a State guarantee to back liquidity facilities provided by a central bank according to the central bank’s conditions, or the form of a State guarantee of newly issued liabilities;
### (b)
the State guarantees referred to in point (a) of this paragraph are required to remedy a serious disturbance in the economy of a Member State and preserve financial stability; and
### (c)
the State guarantees referred to in point (a) of this paragraph are confined to solvent *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCPs]]*, conditional on final approval under the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(39)|Union State aid framework]]*, are of a precautionary and temporary nature, proportionate to remedy the consequences of the serious disturbance referred to in point (b) of this paragraph and are not used to offset losses that the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* has incurred or is likely to incur in the future.
## 5.
The *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(3)|resolution authority]]* may also take a *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(11)|resolution action]]* where it considers that the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* has applied or intends to apply recovery measures which could prevent the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]*’s failure but cause significant adverse effects to the financial system of the Union or of one of more of its Member States.
## 6.
ESMA shall issue guidelines to promote the convergence of supervisory and resolution practices regarding the application of the circumstances under which a *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* is deemed to be failing or likely to fail by 12 February 2022, taking into consideration, as appropriate, the nature, and complexity of the services provided by *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCPs]]* established in the Union.
When developing those guidelines, ESMA shall take into account the guidelines issued in accordance with [[2014L0059_EN.32#6|Article 32(6) of Directive 2014/59/EU]].
