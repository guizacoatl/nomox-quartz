# Article 25 - Portfolio rules for standard MMFs

## 1.
A *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(15)|standard MMF]]* shall comply on an ongoing basis with all of the following requirements:
### (a)
its portfolio is to have at all times a *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(19)|WAM]]* of no more than 6 months;
### (b)
its portfolio is to have at all times a *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(20)|WAL]]* of no more than 12 months, subject to the second and third subparagraphs;
### (c)
at least 7,5 % of its assets are to be comprised of daily maturing assets, *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(5)|reverse repurchase agreements]]* which can be terminated by giving prior notice of one working day or cash which can be withdrawn by giving prior notice of one working day. A *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(15)|standard MMF]]* is not to acquire any asset other than a daily maturing asset when such acquisition would result in that *[[2017R1131_EN.2#2.|MMF]]* investing less than 7,5 % of its portfolio in daily maturing assets;
### (d)
at least 15 % of its assets are to be comprised of weekly maturing assets, *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(5)|reverse repurchase agreements]]* which can be terminated by giving prior notice of five working days or cash which can be withdrawn by giving prior notice of five working days. A *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(15)|standard MMF]]* is not to acquire any asset other than a weekly maturing asset when such acquisition would result in that *[[2017R1131_EN.2#2.|MMF]]* investing less than 15 % of its portfolio in weekly maturing assets;
### (e)
for the purpose of the calculation referred to in point (d), *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(2)|money market instruments]]* or units or shares of other *[[2017R1131_EN.2#2.|MMFs]]* may be included within the weekly maturing assets up to 7,5 % of its assets provided they are able to be redeemed and settled within five working days.

For the purposes of point (b) of the first subparagraph, when calculating the *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(20)|WAL]]* for securities, including structured financial instruments, a *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(15)|standard MMF]]* shall base the maturity calculation on the *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(21)|residual maturity]]* until the legal redemption of the instruments. However, in the event that a financial instrument embeds a put option, a *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(15)|standard MMF]]* may base the maturity calculation on the exercise date of the put option instead of the *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(21)|residual maturity]]*, but only if all of the following conditions are fulfilled at all times:
#### (i)
the put option is able to be freely exercised by the *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(15)|standard MMF]]* at its exercise date;
#### (ii)
the strike price of the put option remains close to the expected value of the instrument at the exercise date;
#### (iii)
the investment strategy of the *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(15)|standard MMF]]* implies that there is a high probability that the option will be exercised at the exercise date.

By way of derogation from the second subparagraph, when calculating the *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(20)|WAL]]* for *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(7)|securitisations]]* and ABCPs, a *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(15)|standard MMF]]* may instead, in the case of amortising instruments, base the maturity calculation on one of the following:
#### (i)
the contractual amortisation profile of such instruments;
#### (ii)
the amortisation profile of the underlying assets from which the cash-flows for the redemption of such instruments result.
## 2.
If the limits referred to in this Article are exceeded for reasons beyond the control of a *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(15)|standard MMF]]* or as a result of the exercise of subscription or redemption rights, that *[[2017R1131_EN.2#2.|MMF]]* shall adopt as a priority objective the correction of that situation, taking due account of the interests of its unit holders or shareholders.
## 3.
A *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(15)|standard MMF]]* shall not take the form of a *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(11)|public debt CNAV MMF]]* or a *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(12)|LVNAV MMF]]*.
