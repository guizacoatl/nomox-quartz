# Article 10 - Eligible money market instruments

## 1.
A money market instrument shall be eligible for investment by an *[[2017R1131_EN.2#2.|MMF]]* provided that it fulfils all of the following requirements:
### (a)
it falls within one of the categories of *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(2)|money market instruments]]* referred to in point (a), (b), (c) or (h) of Article 50(1) of Directive 2009/65/EC;
### (b)
it displays one of the following alternative characteristics:
#### (i)
it has a *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(18)|legal maturity]]* at issuance of 397 days or less;
#### (ii)
it has a *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(21)|residual maturity]]* of 397 days or less;
### (c)
the issuer of the money market instrument and the quality of the money market instrument have received a favourable assessment pursuant to Articles [[2017R1131_EN.19|19]] to 22;
### (d)
where an *[[2017R1131_EN.2#2.|MMF]]* invests in a *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(7)|securitisation]]* or ABCP, it is subject to the requirements laid down in [[2017R1131_EN.11|Article 11]].
## 2.
Notwithstanding point (b) of paragraph 1, *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(15)|standard MMFs]]* shall also be allowed to invest in *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(2)|money market instruments]]* with a *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(21)|residual maturity]]* until the legal redemption date of less than or equal to 2 years, provided that the time remaining until the next interest rate reset date is 397 days or less. For that purpose, floating-rate money-market instruments and fixed-rate money-market instruments hedged by a swap arrangement shall be reset to a money market rate or index.
## 3.
Point (c) of paragraph 1 shall not apply to *[[2017R1131_EN.2#(2)|money market instruments]]* issued or guaranteed by the Union, a central authority or central bank of a Member State, the European Central Bank, the European Investment Bank, the European Stability Mechanism or the European Financial Stability Facility.
