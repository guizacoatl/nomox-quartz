# Article 15 - Assessment of resolvability

## 1.
The *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(3)|resolution authority]]*, in coordination with the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(2)|resolution college]]* in accordance with the procedure set out in [[2021R0023_EN.17|Article 17]], and after consultation with the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(7)|competent authority]]*, shall assess the extent to which a *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* is resolvable without assuming any of the following:
### (a)
*[[2021R0023_EN.2#(30)|extraordinary public financial support]]*;
### (b)
central bank emergency liquidity assistance;
### (c)
central bank liquidity assistance provided under non-standard collateralisation, tenor and interest rate terms.
## 2.
A *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* shall be deemed resolvable where the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(3)|resolution authority]]* considers it feasible and credible to either liquidate it under *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(32)|normal insolvency proceedings]]* or to resolve it applying the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(4)|resolution tools]]* and exercising the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(5)|resolution powers]]* while ensuring the continuity of the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]*’s *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(27)|critical functions]]* and avoiding any use of *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(30)|extraordinary public financial support]]* and, to the maximum extent possible, any significant adverse effect on the financial system and the potential for undue disadvantage to affected stakeholders.
The adverse effects referred to in the first subparagraph shall include broader financial instability or system wide events in any Member State.

The *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(3)|resolution authority]]* shall notify ESMA in a timely manner where it considers that a *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* is not resolvable.
## 3.
Upon request by the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(3)|resolution authority]]*, a *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* shall demonstrate that:
### (a)
there are no impediments to the reduction of the value of *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(33)|instruments of ownership]]* following the exercise of *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(5)|resolution powers]]*, regardless of whether outstanding contractual arrangements or other measures in the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]*’s *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(22)|recovery plan]]* have been fully exhausted; and
### (b)
the contracts of the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* with *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(12)|clearing members]]* or third parties do not enable those *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(12)|clearing members]]* or third parties to successfully challenge the exercise of *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(5)|resolution powers]]* by a *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(3)|resolution authority]]* or otherwise avoid being subject to those powers.
## 4.
For the purposes of the assessment of resolvability referred to in paragraph 1, the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(3)|resolution authority]]* shall, as relevant, examine the matters specified in Section C of the Annex.
## 5.
By 12 August 2022, ESMA, in close cooperation with the ESRB, shall issue guidelines to promote the convergence of resolution practices regarding the application of Section C of the Annex to this Regulation in accordance with [[2010R1095_EN.16|Article 16 of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010]].
## 6.
The *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(3)|resolution authority]]* in coordination with the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(2)|resolution college]]* shall make the resolvability assessment at the same time as drawing up and updating the *[[2021R0023_EN.2#(10)|resolution plan]]* in accordance with [[2021R0023_EN.12|Article 12]].
