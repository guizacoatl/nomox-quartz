# Article 2 - Scope

## 1.
This Regulation applies to natural and legal persons and certain other undertakings that are engaged in the issuance, *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(12)|offer to the public]]* and admission to trading of *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* or that provide services related to *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* in the Union.
## 2.
This Regulation does not apply to:
### (a)
persons who provide *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(16)|crypto-asset services]]* exclusively for their parent companies, for their own subsidiaries or for other subsidiaries of their parent companies;
### (b)
a liquidator or an administrator acting in the course of an insolvency procedure, except for the purposes of [[2023R1114_EN.47|Article 47]];
### (c)
the ECB, central banks of the Member States when acting in their capacity as monetary authorities, or other public authorities of the Member States;
### (d)
the European Investment Bank and its subsidiaries;
### (e)
the European Financial Stability Facility and the European Stability Mechanism;
### (f)
public international organisations.
## 3.
This Regulation does not apply to *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* that are unique and not fungible with other *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]*.
## 4.
This Regulation does not apply to *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* that qualify as one or more of the following:
### (a)
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(49)|financial instruments]]*;
### (b)
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(50)|deposits]]*, including *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(51)|structured deposits]]*;
### (c)
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(14)|funds]]*, except if they qualify as *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(7)|e-money tokens]]*;
### (d)
securitisation positions in the context of a securitisation as defined in [[2023R1114_EN.2|Article 2]], point (1), of Regulation (EU) 2017/2402;
### (e)
non-life or life insurance products falling within the classes of insurance listed in Annexes I and II to Directive 2009/138/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council or reinsurance and retrocession contracts referred to in that Directive;
### (f)
pension products that, under national law, are recognised as having the primary purpose of providing the investor with an income in retirement and that entitle the investor to certain benefits;
### (g)
officially recognised occupational pension schemes falling within the scope of Directive (EU) 2016/2341 of the European Parliament and of the Council or Directive 2009/138/EC;
### (h)
individual pension products for which a financial contribution from the employer is required by national law and where the employer or the employee has no choice as to the pension product or provider;
#### (i)
a pan-European Personal Pension Product as defined in [[2023R1114_EN.2|Article 2]], point (2), of Regulation (EU) 2019/1238 of the European Parliament and of the Council;
### (j)
social security schemes covered by Regulations (EC) No 883/2004 and (EC) No 987/2009 of the European Parliament and of the Council (
## 5
).
## 5.
By 30 December 2024, ESMA shall, for the purposes of paragraph 4, point (a), of this Article issue guidelines in accordance with [[2010R1095_EN.16|Article 16 of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010]] on the conditions and criteria for the qualification of *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* as *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(49)|financial instruments]]*.
## 6.
This Regulation shall be without prejudice to Regulation (EU) No 1024/2013.
