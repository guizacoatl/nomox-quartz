# Article 76 - Operation of a trading platform for crypto-assets

## 1.
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|Crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* shall lay down, maintain and implement clear and transparent operating rules for the trading platform. Those operating rules shall at least:
### (a)
set the approval processes, including customer due diligence requirements commensurate to the money laundering or terrorist financing risk presented by the applicant in accordance with Directive (EU) 2015/849, that are applied before admitting *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* to the trading platform;
### (b)
define exclusion categories, if any, of the types of *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* that are not admitted to trading;
### (c)
set out the policies, procedures and the level of fees, if any, for the admission to trading;
### (d)
set objective, non-discriminatory rules and proportionate criteria for participation in the trading activities, which promote fair and open access to the trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(39)|clients]]* willing to trade;
### (e)
set non-discretionary rules and procedures to ensure fair and orderly trading and objective criteria for the efficient execution of orders;
### (f)
set conditions for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* to remain accessible for trading, including liquidity thresholds and periodic disclosure requirements;
### (g)
set conditions under which trading of *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* can be suspended;
### (h)
set procedures to ensure efficient settlement of both *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* and *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(14)|funds]]*.

For the purposes of point (a) of the first subparagraph, the operating rules shall clearly state that a *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-asset]]* is not to be admitted to trading where no corresponding *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-asset]]* white paper has been published in the cases required by this Regulation.
## 2.
Before admitting a *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-asset]]* to trading, *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* shall ensure that the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-asset]]* complies with the operating rules of the trading platform and shall assess the suitability of the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-asset]]* concerned. When assessing the suitability of a *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-asset]]*, the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform shall evaluate, in particular, the reliability of the technical solutions used and the potential association to illicit or fraudulent activities, taking into account the experience, track record and reputation of the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(10)|issuer]]* of those *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* and its development team. The *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform shall also assess the suitability of the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* other than *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(6)|asset-referenced tokens]]* or *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(7)|e-money tokens]]* referred to in [[2023R1114_EN.4#3|Article 4(3)]], first subparagraph, points (a) to (d).
## 3.
The operating rules of the trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* shall prevent the admission to trading of *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* that have an inbuilt anonymisation function unless the holders of those *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* and their transaction history can be identified by the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]*.
## 4.
The operating rules referred to in paragraph 1 shall be drawn up in an official language of the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(33)|home Member State]]*, or in a language customary in the sphere of international finance.

If the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(18)|operation of a trading platform for crypto-assets]]* is provided in another Member State, the operating rules referred to in paragraph 1 shall be drawn up in an official language of the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(34)|host Member State]]*, or in a language customary in the sphere of international finance.
## 5.
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|Crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* shall not deal on own account on the trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* they operate, including where they provide the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(19)|exchange of crypto-assets for funds]]* or other *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]*.
## 6.
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|Crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* shall only be allowed to engage in *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(40)|matched principal trading]]* where the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(39)|client]]* has consented to that process. *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|Crypto-asset service providers]]* shall provide the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(35)|competent authority]]* with information explaining their use of *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(40)|matched principal trading]]*. The *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(35)|competent authority]]* shall monitor the engagement of *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|crypto-asset service providers]]* in *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(40)|matched principal trading]]*, and ensure that their engagement in *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(40)|matched principal trading]]* continues to fall within the definition of such trading and does not give rise to conflicts of interest between the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|crypto-asset service providers]]* and their *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(39)|clients]]*.
## 7.
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|Crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* shall have in place effective systems, procedures and arrangements to ensure that their trading systems:
### (a)
are resilient;
### (b)
have sufficient capacity to deal with peak order and message volumes;
### (c)
are able to ensure orderly trading under conditions of severe market stress;
### (d)
are able to reject orders that exceed pre-determined volume and price thresholds or are clearly erroneous;
### (e)
are fully tested to ensure that the conditions under points (a) to (d) are met;
### (f)
are subject to effective business continuity arrangements to ensure the continuity of their services if there is any failure of the trading system;
### (g)
are able to prevent or detect market abuse;
### (h)
are sufficiently robust to prevent their abuse for the purposes of money laundering or terrorist financing.
## 8.
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|Crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* shall inform their *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(35)|competent authority]]* when they identify cases of market abuse or attempted market abuse occurring on or through their trading systems.
## 9.
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|Crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* shall make public any bid and ask prices and the depth of trading interests at those prices which are advertised for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* through their trading platforms. The *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|crypto-asset service providers]]* concerned shall make that information available to the public on a continuous basis during trading hours.
## 10.
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|Crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* shall make public the price, volume and time of the transactions executed in respect of *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* traded on their trading platforms. They shall make those details for all such transactions public as close to real-time as is technically possible.
## 11.
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|Crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* shall make the information published in accordance with paragraphs 9 and 10 available to the public on a reasonable commercial basis and ensure non-discriminatory access to that information. That information shall be made available free of charge 15 minutes after publication in a machine-readable format and it shall remain published for at least two years.
## 12.
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|Crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* shall initiate the final settlement of a *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-asset]]* transaction on the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(2)|distributed ledger]]* within 24 hours of the transaction being executed on the trading platform or, in the case of transactions settled outside the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(2)|distributed ledger]]*, by the closing of the day at the latest.
## 13.
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|Crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* shall ensure that their fee structures are transparent, fair and non-discriminatory and that they do not create incentives to place, modify or cancel orders or to execute transactions in a way that contributes to disorderly trading conditions or market abuse as referred to in Title VI.
## 14.
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|Crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform for *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* shall maintain resources and have back-up facilities in place to enable them to report to their *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(35)|competent authority]]* at all times.
## 15.
*[[2023R1114_EN.3#(15)|Crypto-asset service providers]]* operating a trading platform shall keep at the disposal of the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(35)|competent authority]]*, for at least five years, the relevant data relating to all orders in *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(5)|crypto-assets]]* that are advertised through their systems, or give the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(35)|competent authority]]* access to the order book so that the *[[2023R1114_EN.3#(35)|competent authority]]* is able to monitor the trading activity. That relevant data shall contain the characteristics of the order, including those that link an order with the executed transactions that stem from that order.
## 16.
ESMA shall develop draft regulatory technical standards to further specify:
### (a)
the manner in which transparency data, including the level of disaggregation of the data to be made available to the public as referred to in paragraphs 1, 9 and 10, is to be presented;
### (b)
the content and format of order book records to be maintained as specified in paragraph 15.

ESMA shall submit the draft regulatory technical standards referred to in the first subparagraph to the Commission by 30 June 2024.

Power is delegated to the Commission to supplement this Regulation by adopting the regulatory technical standards referred to in the first subparagraph of this paragraph in accordance with Articles [[2010R1095_EN.10|10]] to [[2010R1095_EN.14|14]] of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010.
