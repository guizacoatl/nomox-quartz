## 1.
For each *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(10)|securities settlement system]]* it operates a *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSD]]* shall have publicly disclosed criteria for *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(20)|participation]]* which allow fair and open access for all legal persons that intend to become *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(19)|participants]]*. Such criteria shall be transparent, objective, and non-discriminatory so as to ensure fair and open access to the *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSD]]* with due regard to risks to financial stability and the orderliness of markets. Criteria that restrict access shall be permitted only to the extent that their objective is to justifiably *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(21)|control]]* a specified risk for the *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSD]]*.
## 2.
A *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSD]]* shall treat requests for access promptly by providing a response to such requests within one month at the latest and shall make the procedures for treating access requests publicly available.
## 3.
A *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSD]]* shall deny access to a *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(19)|participant]]* meeting the criteria referred to in paragraph 1 only where duly justified in writing and based on a comprehensive risk assessment.

In the event of a refusal, the requesting *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(19)|participant]]* has the right to complain to the *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(17)|competent authority]]* of the *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSD]]* that has refused access.

That *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(17)|competent authority]]* shall duly examine the complaint by assessing the reasons for refusal and shall provide the requesting *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(19)|participant]]* with a reasoned reply.

That *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(17)|competent authority]]* shall consult the *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(17)|competent authority]]* of the place of establishment of the requesting *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(19)|participant]]* on its assessment of the complaint. Where the authority of the requesting *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(19)|participant]]* disagrees with the assessment provided, any one of the two competent authorities may refer the matter to ESMA, which may act in accordance with the powers conferred on it under [[2010R1095_EN.19|Article 19 of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010]].

Where the refusal by the *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSD]]* to grant access to the requesting *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(19)|participant]]* is deemed to be unjustified, the *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(17)|competent authority]]* of the *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSD]]* that has refused access shall issue an order requiring that *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSD]]* to grant access to the requesting *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(19)|participant]]*.
## 4.
A *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSD]]* shall have objective and transparent procedures for the suspension and orderly exit of *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(19)|participants]]* that no longer meet the criteria for *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(20)|participation]]* referred to in paragraph 1.
## 5.
ESMA shall, in close cooperation with the members of the ESCB, develop draft regulatory technical standards to specify the risks to be taken into account by *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSDs]]* when carrying out a comprehensive risk assessment, and by competent authorities when assessing the reasons for refusal in accordance with paragraph 3 and the elements of the procedure referred to in paragraph 3.

ESMA shall submit those draft regulatory technical standards to the Commission by 18 June 2015.

Power is delegated to the Commission to adopt the regulatory technical standards referred to in the first subparagraph in accordance with Articles [[2010R1095_EN.10|10]] to [[2010R1095_EN.14|14]] of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010.
## 6.
ESMA shall, in close cooperation with the members of the ESCB, develop draft implementing technical standards to establish standard forms and templates for the procedure referred to in paragraph 3.

ESMA shall submit those draft implementing technical standards to the Commission by 18 June 2015.

Power is conferred on the Commission to adopt the implementing technical standards referred to in the first subparagraph in accordance with [[2010R1095_EN.15|Article 15 of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010]].
