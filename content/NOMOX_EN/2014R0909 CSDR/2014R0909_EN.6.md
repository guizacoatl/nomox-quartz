# Article 6 - Measures to prevent settlement fails

## 1.
*[[2014R0909_EN.2#(42)|Trading venues]]* shall establish procedures that enable the confirmation of relevant details of transactions in *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(8)|financial instruments]]* referred to in [[2014R0909_EN.5#1|Article 5(1)]] on the date when the transaction has been executed.
## 2.
Notwithstanding the requirement laid down in paragraph 1, investment firms authorised pursuant to [[2014L0065_EN.5|Article 5 of Directive 2014/65/EU]] shall, where applicable, take measures to limit the number of *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(15)|settlement fails]]*.

Such measures shall at least consist of arrangements between the investment firm and its professional clients as referred to in Annex II to Directive 2014/65/EU to ensure the prompt communication of an allocation of *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(8)|securities]]* to the transaction, confirmation of that allocation and confirmation of the acceptance or rejection of terms in good time before the *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(12)|intended settlement date]]*.

ESMA shall, in close cooperation with the members of the ESCB, issue guidelines in accordance with [[2010R1095_EN.16|Article 16 of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010]] on the standardised procedures and messaging protocols to be used for complying with the second subparagraph of this paragraph.
## 3.
For each *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(10)|securities settlement system]]* it operates, a *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSD]]* shall establish procedures that facilitate the *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(7)|settlement]]* of transactions in *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(8)|financial instruments]]* referred to in [[2014R0909_EN.5#1|Article 5(1)]] on the *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(12)|intended settlement date]]* with a minimum exposure of its *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(19)|participants]]* to counterparty and liquidity risks and a low rate of *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(15)|settlement fails]]*. It shall promote early *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(7)|settlement]]* on the *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(12)|intended settlement date]]* through appropriate mechanisms.
## 4.
For each *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(10)|securities settlement system]]* it operates, a *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSD]]* shall put in place measures to encourage and incentivise the timely *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(7)|settlement]]* of transactions by its *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(19)|participants]]*. *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSDs]]* shall require *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(19)|participants]]* to settle their transactions on the *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(12)|intended settlement date]]*.
## 5.
ESMA shall, in close cooperation with the members of the ESCB, develop draft regulatory technical standards to specify the measures to prevent *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(15)|settlement fails]]* in order to increase *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(7)|settlement]]* efficiency and in particular:
### (a)
the measures to be taken by investment firms in accordance with paragraph 2, first subparagraph;
### (b)
the details of the procedures that facilitate *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(7)|settlement]]* referred to in paragraph 3, which could include the shaping of transaction sizes, partial *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(7)|settlement]]* of failing trades and the use of auto-lend/borrow programmes provided by certain *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(1)|CSDs]]*; and
### (c)
the details of the measures to encourage and incentivise the timely *[[2014R0909_EN.2#(7)|settlement]]* of transactions referred to in paragraph 4.

ESMA shall submit those draft regulatory technical standards to the Commission by 17 July 2025.

Power is delegated to the Commission to adopt the regulatory technical standards referred to in the first subparagraph in accordance with Articles [[2010R1095_EN.10|10]] to [[2010R1095_EN.14|14]] of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010.
