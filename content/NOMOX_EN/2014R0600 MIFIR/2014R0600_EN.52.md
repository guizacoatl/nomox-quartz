# Article 52 - Reports and review

## 1.
By
3 March 2020

, the Commission shall, after consulting ESMA, submit a report to the European Parliament and to the Council on the impact in practice of the transparency obligations established pursuant to Articles [[2014R0600_EN.3|3]] to 13, in particular on the impact of the volume cap mechanism described in [[2014R0600_EN.5|Article 5]], including on the cost of trading for eligible counterparties and *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(8)|professional clients]]* and on trading of shares of small and mid-cap companies, and its effectiveness in ensuring that the use of the relevant waivers does not harm price formation and how any appropriate mechanism for imposing sanctions for infringements of the volume cap might operate, and on the application and continued appropriateness of the waivers to pre-trade transparency obligations established pursuant to [[2014R0600_EN.4#2|Article 4(2)]] and (3) and [[2014R0600_EN.9#2|Article 9(2)]] to (5).
## 2.
The report referred to in paragraph 1 shall include the impact on European equity markets of the use of the waiver under [[2014R0600_EN.4#1#a|Article 4(1)(a)]] and (b)(i) and the volume cap mechanism under [[2014R0600_EN.5|Article 5]], with particular reference to:
### (a)
the level and trend of non-lit order book trading within the Union since the introduction of this Regulation;
### (b)
the impact on the pre-trade transparent quoted spreads;
### (c)
the impact on the depth of liquidity on lit order books;
### (d)
the impact on competition and on investors within the Union;
### (e)
the impact on trading of shares of small and mid-cap companies;
### (f)
developments at international level and discussions with third countries and international organisations.
## 3.
If the report concludes that the use of the waiver under [[2014R0600_EN.4#1#a|Article 4(1)(a)]] and (b)(i) is harmful to price formation or to trading of shares of small and mid-cap companies, the Commission shall, where appropriate, make proposals, including amendments to this Regulation, regarding the use of those waivers. Such proposals shall include an impact assessment of the proposed amendments, and shall take into account the objectives of this Regulation and the effects on market disruption and competition, and potential impacts on investors in the Union.
## 4.
By
3 March 2020

, the Commission shall, after consulting ESMA, submit a report to the European Parliament and to the Council on the functioning of [[2014R0600_EN.26|Article 26]], including whether the content and format of transaction reports received and exchanged between competent authorities comprehensively enable to monitor the activities of *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firms]]* in accordance with [[2014R0600_EN.26#1|Article 26(1)]]. The Commission may make any appropriate proposals, including providing for transactions to be reported to a system appointed by ESMA instead of to competent authorities, which allows relevant competent authorities to access all the information reported pursuant to this Article for the purposes of this Regulation and of Directive 2014/65/EU and the detection of insider dealing and market abuse in accordance with Regulation (EU) No 596/2014.
## 5.
By
3 March 2020

, the Commission shall, after consulting ESMA, submit a report to the European Parliament and to the Council on appropriate solutions to reduce information asymmetries between market participants as well as tools for regulators to better monitor quotation activities on *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venues]]*. That report shall at least assess the feasibility of developing a European best bid and offer system for consolidated quotes to fulfil those objectives.
## 6.
By
3 March 2020

, the Commission shall, after consulting ESMA, submit a report to the European Parliament and to the Council on the progress made in moving trading in standardised *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(32a)|OTC derivatives]]* to exchanges or electronic trading platforms pursuant to Articles [[2014R0600_EN.25|25]] and [[2014R0600_EN.28|28]].
## 7.
By
3 July 2020

, the Commission shall, after consulting ESMA, submit a report to the European Parliament and to the Council on the development in prices for pre-trade and post-trade transparency data from *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(13)|regulated markets]]*, *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(14)|MTFs]]*, *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(15)|OTFs]]*, *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(34)|APAs]]* and *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(35)|CTPs]]*.
## 8.
By
3 July 2020

, the Commission shall, after consulting ESMA, submit a report to the European Parliament and to the Council reviewing the interoperability provisions in [[2014R0600_EN.36|Article 36]] of this Regulation and of [[2012R0648_EN.8|Article 8 of Regulation (EU) No 648/2012]].
## 9.
By
3 July 2020

, the Commission shall, after consulting ESMA, submit a report to the European Parliament and to the Council on the application of Articles [[2014R0600_EN.35|35]] and [[2014R0600_EN.36|36]] of this Regulation and of Articles [[2014R0600_EN.7|7]] and [[2014R0600_EN.8|8]] of Regulation (EU) No 648/2012.

By
3 July 2022

, the Commission shall, after consulting ESMA, submit a report to the European Parliament and to the Council on the application of [[2014R0600_EN.37|Article 37]].
## 10.
By
3 July 2020

, the Commission shall, after consulting ESMA, submit a report to the European Parliament and to the Council on the impact of [[2014R0600_EN.35|Article 35]] and 36 of this Regulation on newly established and authorised *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCPs]]* as referred to in [[2014R0600_EN.35#5|Article 35(5)]] and *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venues]]* connected to those *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCPs]]* by *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(21)|close links]]* and whether the transitional arrangement provided for in [[2014R0600_EN.35#5|Article 35(5)]] shall be extended, weighing the possible benefits to consumers of improving competition and the degree of choice available to market participants against the possible disproportionate effect of those provisions on newly established and authorised *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCPs]]* and the constraints of local market participants in accessing global *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCPs]]* and the smooth functioning of the market.

Subject to the conclusions of that report, the Commission may adopt a delegated act in accordance with [[2014R0600_EN.50|Article 50]] to extend the transitional period in accordance with [[2014R0600_EN.35#5|Article 35(5)]] by a maximum of 30 months.

## 14.
By 30 June 2026, ESMA, in close cooperation with the expert stakeholder group established pursuant to [[2014R0600_EN.22b#2|Article 22b(2)]], shall assess the market demand for the consolidated tape for shares and *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(26)|ETFs]]*, the impact of that consolidated tape on the functioning, attractiveness and international competitiveness of Union markets and firms, and whether the consolidated tape has delivered on its aim to decrease information asymmetries between market participants and to make the Union a more attractive location to invest. ESMA shall report to the Commission on the appropriateness of adding additional features to the consolidated tape, such as the dissemination of the market identifier code for pre-trade data. On the basis of that report, the Commission shall submit, where appropriate, a legislative proposal to the European Parliament and the Council.
## 14a.
Three years after the first authorisation of a consolidated tape, the Commission shall, after consulting ESMA and the expert stakeholder group established pursuant to [[2014R0600_EN.22b#2|Article 22b(2)]], submit a report to the European Parliament and to the Council on the following:
### (a)
the asset classes covered by a consolidated tape;
### (b)
the timeliness and the quality of the data transmitted to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(35)|CTP]]*;
### (c)
the timeliness of the dissemination, and the quality, of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36b)|core market data]]* and *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36c)|regulatory data]]*;
### (d)
the role of *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36b)|core market data]]* and *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36c)|regulatory data]]* in reducing implementation shortfall;
### (e)
the number of users of the consolidated tape per asset class;
### (f)
the effect of *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36b)|core market data]]* and *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36c)|regulatory data]]* on remedying information asymmetries between various capital market participants;
### (g)
the appropriateness of the transmission protocols used for the transmission of data to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(35)|CTP]]*;
### (h)
the appropriateness and functioning of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#6.|revenue redistribution scheme]]*, in particular as regards *[[2014R0600_EN.2#1.|data contributors]]* that are small *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venues]]*;
#### (i)
the effects of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36b)|core market data]]* and *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36c)|regulatory data]]* on investments in SMEs.
## 14b.
By 29 March 2025, the Commission shall, in close cooperation with ESMA, assess the possibility of extending the requirements of [[2014R0600_EN.26|Article 26]] of this Regulation to AIFMs as defined in [[2014R0600_EN.4#1|Article 4(1)]], point (b), of Directive 2011/61/EU, and management companies, as defined in [[2014R0600_EN.2#1|Article 2(1)]], point (b), of Directive 2009/65/EC, which provide *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(2)|investment services and activities]]* and which execute transactions in *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(9)|financial instruments]]*. In particular, the Commission shall include in that assessment a cost-benefit analysis and an evaluation of the scope of such extension.

On the basis of that assessment and taking into account the goals of the capital markets union, the Commission is empowered to adopt delegated acts in accordance with [[2014R0600_EN.50|Article 50]] to amend this Regulation by extending the requirements of [[2014R0600_EN.26|Article 26]] in accordance with the first subparagraph of this paragraph.
## 14c.
By 29 March 2028, ESMA shall submit to the Commission a report assessing the appropriateness of the volume cap set in [[2014R0600_EN.5#1|Article 5(1)]] and the necessity to remove or to extend it to other trading systems or execution venues which derive their prices from a reference price, taking into account international best practices, the competitiveness of Union financial markets, and the effects of that volume cap on the fair and orderly trading on markets, and on the efficiency of price formation.
## 15.
The Commission is empowered to adopt delegated acts in accordance with [[2014R0600_EN.50|Article 50]] to supplement this Regulation, by specifying measures in order to:
### (e)
ensure that the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36b)|core market data]]* and *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36c)|regulatory data]]* are provided on a reasonable commercial basis and meet the needs of the users of those data across the Union;

—————
### (g)
specify arrangements applicable where the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(35)|CTP]]* no longer fulfils the selection criteria;
### (h)
specify arrangements under which a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(35)|CTP]]* may continue to operate a consolidated tape provided that no new entity is authorised through the selection procedure.
