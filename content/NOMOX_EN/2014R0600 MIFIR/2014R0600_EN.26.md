# Article 26 - Obligation to report transactions

## 1.
*[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|Investment firms]]* which execute transactions in *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(9)|financial instruments]]* shall report complete and accurate details of such transactions to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* as quickly as possible, and no later than the close of the following working day.

The competent authorities shall, in accordance with [[2014L0065_EN.85|Article 85 of Directive 2014/65/EU]], establish the necessary arrangements in order to ensure that the following competent authorities also receive that information:
### (a)
the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* of the most relevant market in terms of liquidity for those *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(9)|financial instruments]]*;
### (b)
the competent authorities responsible for the supervision of the transmitting *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firms]]*;
### (c)
the competent authorities responsible for the supervision of the branches which have been part of the transaction; and
### (d)
the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* responsible for the supervision of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venues]]* used.

The *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* referred to in the first subparagraph shall without undue delay make available to ESMA any information reported in accordance with this Article.
## 2.
The obligation laid down in paragraph 1 shall apply to:
### (a)
*[[2014R0600_EN.2#(9)|financial instruments]]* which are admitted to trading or traded on a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* or for which a request for admission to trading has been made, irrespective of whether such transactions are carried out on the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]*, with the exception of transactions in *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(32a)|OTC derivatives]]* other than those referred in [[2014R0600_EN.8a#2|Article 8a(2)]], to which the obligation shall apply only when carried out on a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]*;
### (b)
*[[2014R0600_EN.2#(9)|financial instruments]]* where the underlying is a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(9)|financial instrument]]* that is traded on a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]*, irrespective of whether such transactions are carried out on the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]*;
### (c)
*[[2014R0600_EN.2#(9)|financial instruments]]* where the underlying is an index or a basket composed of *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(9)|financial instruments]]* that are traded on a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]*, irrespective of whether such transactions are carried out on the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]*;
### (d)
*[[2014R0600_EN.2#(32a)|OTC derivatives]]* as referred to in [[2014R0600_EN.8a#2|Article 8a(2)]], irrespective of whether such transactions are carried out on the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]*.
## 3.
The reports shall, in particular, include details of the names and numbers of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(9)|financial instruments]]* bought or sold, the quantity, the dates and times of execution, the effective dates, the transaction prices, a designation to identify the parties on whose behalf the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]* has executed that transaction, a designation to identify the persons and the computer algorithms within the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]* responsible for the investment decision and the execution of the transaction, a designation to identify the entity subject to the reporting obligation, and means of identifying the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firms]]* concerned. Reports on a transaction made at the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* shall include a transaction identification code generated and disseminated by the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* to both buying and selling members of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]*.

For transactions not carried out on a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]*, the reports shall include a designation identifying the types of transactions in accordance with the regulatory technical standards adopted pursuant to [[2014R0600_EN.20#3|Article 20(3)]], point (a), and [[2014R0600_EN.21#5|Article 21(5)]], point (a), of this Regulation. For *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(30)|commodity derivatives]]*, the reports shall indicate whether the transaction reduces risk in an objectively measurable way in accordance with [[2014L0065_EN.57|Article 57 of Directive 2014/65/EU]].
## 4.
*[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|Investment firms]]* which transmit orders shall include in the transmission of that order all the details as specified in paragraphs 1 and 3. Instead of including the mentioned details when transmitting orders, an *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]* may choose to report the transmitted order, if it is executed, as a transaction in accordance with the requirements under paragraph 1. In that case, the transaction report by the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]* shall state that it pertains to a transmitted order.
## 5.
The operator of a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* shall report details of transactions in *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(9)|financial instruments]]* traded on its platform which are executed through its systems by any member, participant or user not subject to this Regulation in accordance with paragraphs 1 and 3.
## 6.
In reporting the designation to identify the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(7)|clients]]* as required under paragraphs 3 and 4, *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firms]]* shall use a legal entity identifier established to identify *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(7)|clients]]* that are legal persons.

ESMA shall develop by 3 January 2016 guidelines in accordance with [[2010R1095_EN.16|Article 16 of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010]] to ensure that the application of legal entity identifiers within the Union complies with international standards, in particular those established by the Financial Stability Board.
## 7.
The reports shall be made to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* either by the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]* itself, an *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36)|ARM]]* acting on its behalf or by the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* through whose system the transaction was completed, in accordance with paragraphs 1, 3 and 9.

*[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|Investment firms]]* shall have responsibility for the completeness, accuracy and timely submission of the reports which are submitted to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]*.

By way of derogation from that responsibility, where an *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]* reports details of those transactions through an *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36)|ARM]]* which is acting on its behalf or a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]*, the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]* shall not be responsible for failures in the completeness, accuracy or timely submission of the reports which are attributable to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36)|ARM]]* or *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]*. In those cases and subject to [[2014L0065_EN.66#4|Article 66(4) of Directive 2014/65/EU]] the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36)|ARM]]* or *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* shall be responsible for those failures.

*[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|Investment firms]]* must nevertheless take reasonable steps to verify the completeness, accuracy and timeliness of the transaction reports which were submitted on their behalf.

The *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(37)|home Member State]]* shall require the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]*, when making reports on behalf of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]*, to have sound security mechanisms in place designed to guarantee the security and authentication of the means of transfer of information, to minimise the risk of data corruption and unauthorised access and to prevent information leakage maintaining the confidentiality of the data at all times. The *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(37)|home Member State]]* shall require the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* to maintain adequate resources and have back-up facilities in place in order to offer and maintain its services at all times.

Trade-matching or reporting systems, including trade repositories registered or recognised in accordance with Title VI of Regulation (EU) No 648/2012, may be approved by the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* as an *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36)|ARM]]* in order to transmit transaction reports to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* in accordance with paragraphs 1, 3 and 9.

Where transactions have been reported to a trade repository in accordance with [[2012R0648_EN.9|Article 9 of Regulation (EU) No 648/2012]] which is approved as an *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36)|ARM]]* and where those reports contain the details required under paragraphs 1, 3 and 9 and are transmitted to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* by the trade repository within the time limit set in paragraph 1, the obligation on the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]* laid down in paragraph 1 shall be considered to have been complied with.

Where there are errors or omissions in the transaction reports, the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(36)|ARM]]*, *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]* or *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* reporting the transaction shall correct the information and submit a corrected report to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]*.
## 8.
An *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]* shall report transactions executed wholly or partly through its *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(20)|branch]]* to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(37)|home Member State]]* of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]*. The *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(20)|branch]]* of a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(42)|third country firm]]* shall submit its transaction reports to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* which authorised the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(20)|branch]]*. Where a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(42)|third-country firm]]* has set up branches in more than one Member State, those branches shall determine the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* that is to receive all the transaction reports.

When, in accordance with [[2014L0065_EN.35#8|Article 35(8) of Directive 2014/65/EU]], reports provided for under this Article are transmitted to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(38)|host Member State]]*, it shall transmit that information to the competent authorities of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(37)|home Member State]]* of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]*, unless the competent authorities of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(37)|home Member State]]* decide that they do not want to receive that information.
## 9.
ESMA shall develop draft regulatory technical standards to specify:
### (a)
data standards and formats for the information to be reported in accordance with paragraphs 1 and 3, including the methods and arrangements for reporting financial transactions and the form and content of such reports;
### (b)
the criteria for defining a relevant market in accordance with paragraph 1;
### (c)
the references of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(9)|financial instruments]]* bought or sold, the quantity, the dates and times of execution, the effective dates, the transaction prices, the information and details of the identity of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(7)|client]]*, a designation to identify the parties on whose behalf the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]* has executed that transaction, a designation to identify the persons and the computer algorithms within the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]* responsible for the investment decision and the execution of the transaction, a designation to identify the entity subject to the reporting obligation, the means of identifying the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firms]]* concerned, the way in which the transaction was executed, data fields necessary for the processing and analysis of the transaction reports in accordance with paragraph 3;

—————
### (e)
the relevant categories of indices to be reported in accordance with paragraph 2, point (c);
### (f)
the conditions upon which legal entity identifiers are developed, attributed and maintained, by Member States in accordance with paragraph 6, and the conditions under which those legal entity identifiers are used by *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firms]]* so as to provide, pursuant to paragraphs 3, 4 and 5, for the designation to identify the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(7)|clients]]* in the transaction reports they are required to establish pursuant to paragraph 1;
### (g)
the application of transaction reporting obligations to branches of *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firms]]*;
### (h)
what constitutes a transaction and execution of a transaction for the purposes of this Article;
#### (i)
when an *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firm]]* is deemed to have transmitted an order for the purposes of paragraph 4;
### (j)
the conditions for linking specific transactions and the means of identifying aggregated orders resulting in the execution of a transaction; and
### (k)
the date by which transactions are to be reported.

When developing those draft regulatory technical standards, ESMA shall take into account international developments and standards agreed at Union or international level, and the consistency of those draft regulatory technical standards with the reporting requirements laid down in Regulations (EU) No 648/2012 and (EU) 2015/2365.

ESMA shall submit the draft regulatory technical standards referred to in the first subparagraph to the Commission by 29 September 2025.

Power is delegated to the Commission to supplement this Regulation by adopting the regulatory technical standards referred to in the first subparagraph in accordance with Articles [[2010R1095_EN.10|10]] to [[2010R1095_EN.14|14]] of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010.
## 10.
By
3 January 2020

, ESMA shall submit a report to the Commission on the functioning of this Article, including its interaction with the related reporting obligations under Regulation (EU) No 648/2012, and whether the content and format of transaction reports received and exchanged between competent authorities comprehensively enables monitoring of the activities of *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(1)|investment firms]]* in accordance with [[2014R0600_EN.24|Article 24]] of this Regulation. The Commission may take steps to propose any changes, including providing for transactions to be transmitted only to a single system appointed by ESMA instead of to competent authorities. The Commission shall forward ESMA’s report to the European Parliament and to the Council.
## 11.
By 29 March 2028, ESMA shall submit to the Commission a report assessing the feasibility of more integration in transaction reporting and streamlining of data flows pursuant to this Article to:
### (a)
reduce duplicative or inconsistent requirements for transaction data reporting, and in particular duplicative or inconsistent requirements laid down in this Regulation and Regulations (EU) No 648/2012 and (EU) 2015/2365, and in other relevant Union legal acts;
### (b)
improve data standardisation and efficient sharing and use of data reported within any Union reporting framework by any relevant authority at Union or national level.

When preparing the report, ESMA shall, where relevant, work in close cooperation with the other bodies of the European System of Financial Supervision and the European Central Bank.
