# Article 32a - Stand-alone suspension of the trading obligation

## 1.
At the request of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* of a Member State, the Commission may, by way of an implementing act, suspend the trading obligation laid down in [[2014R0600_EN.28|Article 28]] (the ‘*[[2014R0600_EN.2#1.|derivative trading obligation]]*’) with respect to certain financial counterparties, where appropriate after consulting ESMA. The *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* shall indicate why it considers that the conditions for a suspension are met. In particular, the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* shall demonstrate that a financial counterparty within its jurisdiction:
### (a)
regularly acts as a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(6)|market maker]]* in an *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(32a)|OTC derivative]]* subject to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#1.|derivative trading obligation]]* and regularly receives requests for a quote for the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(29)|derivatives]]* subject to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#1.|derivative trading obligation]]* from a non-EEA counterparty which has no active membership on an EEA *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* that offers trading in the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(32a)|OTC derivative]]* subject to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#1.|derivative trading obligation]]*; or
### (b)
regularly acts as a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(6)|market maker]]* in a credit default swap subject to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#1.|derivative trading obligation]]* and:
#### (i)
intends to trade credit default swaps subject to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#1.|derivative trading obligation]]* on own account on a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* open only to counterparties that are *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* clearing members as defined in [[2014R0600_EN.2|Article 2]], point (14), of Regulation (EU) No 648/2012 (‘dealer-to-dealer’ venue);
#### (ii)
intends to trade credit default swaps subject to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#1.|derivative trading obligation]]* on own account with a counterparty which is a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(6)|market maker]]* and which has no active membership on an EEA dealer-to-dealer venue that offers trading in the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(32a)|OTC derivatives]]* subject to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#1.|derivative trading obligation]]*; and
#### (iii)
clears those credit default swaps in a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* authorised or recognised pursuant to Regulation (EU) No 648/2012.

The implementing act referred to in the first subparagraph of this paragraph shall be adopted in accordance with the examination procedure referred to in [[2014R0600_EN.51|Article 51]].
## 2.
When assessing whether to suspend the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#1.|derivative trading obligation]]* pursuant to paragraph 1, the Commission shall consider whether to suspend it for specific markets, and shall take into account whether such suspension of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#1.|derivative trading obligation]]* would have a distortive effect on the clearing obligation under Title II of Regulation (EU) No 648/2012.

The Commission shall also contact other competent authorities of other Member States to assess whether financial counterparties in Member States other than that making the request pursuant to paragraph 1 (the ‘requesting Member State’) are in a situation similar to that in the requesting Member State.

The *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* of a Member State other than the requesting Member State may, after adoption of the implementing act referred to in paragraph 1, request that financial counterparties that are in a situation similar to that in the requesting Member State be added to the implementing act. The *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* of the Member State making that request shall demonstrate why it considers that the conditions for a suspension are met.
## 3.
Where the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#1.|derivative trading obligation]]* is suspended pursuant to paragraph 1 or 2 with respect to a financial counterparty, the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#1.|derivative trading obligation]]* shall not apply with respect to its counterparty, as referred to in paragraph 1, point (a), or paragraph 1, point (b)(ii).
## 4.
The implementing act referred to in paragraph 1 shall be accompanied by the evidence presented by the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* requesting the suspension.
## 5.
The implementing act referred to in paragraph 1 shall be communicated to ESMA and shall be published in the register referred to in [[2014R0600_EN.34|Article 34]].
## 6.
The Commission shall regularly review whether the grounds for the suspension of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#1.|derivative trading obligation]]* continue to apply.
