# Article 35 - Non-discriminatory access to a CCP

## 1.
Without prejudice to [[2012R0648_EN.7|Article 7 of Regulation (EU) No 648/2012]], a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* shall accept to clear *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(9)|financial instruments]]* on a non-discriminatory and transparent basis, including as regards collateral requirements and fees relating to access, regardless of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* on which a transaction is executed.

The requirement in the first subparagraph shall not apply to *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(32)|exchange-traded derivatives]]*.

The *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* shall in particular ensure that a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* has the right to non-discriminatory treatment of contracts traded on that *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* in terms of:
### (a)
collateral requirements and netting of economically equivalent contracts, where the inclusion of such contracts in the close-out and other netting procedures of a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* based on the applicable insolvency law would not endanger the smooth and orderly functioning, the validity or enforceability of such procedures; and
### (b)
cross-margining with correlated contracts cleared by the same *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* under a risk model that complies with [[2012R0648_EN.41|Article 41 of Regulation (EU) No 648/2012]].

A *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* may require that the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* comply with the operational and technical requirements established by the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* including the risk management requirements. The requirement in this paragraph does not apply to any derivative contract that is already subject to the access obligations under [[2012R0648_EN.7|Article 7 of Regulation (EU) No 648/2012]].

A *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* is not bound by this Article if it is connected by *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(21)|close links]]* to a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* which has given notification under [[2014R0600_EN.36#5|Article 36(5)]].
## 2.
A request to access a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* by a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* shall be formally submitted to a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]*, its relevant *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* and the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]*. The request shall specify to which types of *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(9)|financial instruments]]* access is requested.
## 3.
The *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* shall provide a written response to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* within three months of receiving the request referred to in paragraph 2, either permitting access, provided that the relevant *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* has granted access pursuant to paragraph 4, or denying access. The *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* may deny a request for access subject to the conditions laid down in paragraph 6, point (a). Where a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* denies access, it shall provide full reasons in its response and inform its *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* of the decision in writing. Where the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* is established in a Member State other than that of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]*, the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* shall also forward that written response to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]*. The *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* shall provide access within three months of providing a positive response to the access request.
## 4.
The *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* or that of the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* shall grant a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* access to a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* provided that such access would not threaten the smooth and orderly functioning of the markets, in particular due to *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(45)|liquidity fragmentation]]*, or would not adversely affect systemic risk.

—————

If a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* refuses access it shall issue its decision within two months following receipt of the request referred to in paragraph 2 and provide full reasons to the other *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]*, the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* and the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* including the evidence on which the decision is based.
## 5.
As regards transferable śecurities and money market instruments, a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* that has been newly established and authorised as a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* as defined in [[2012R0648_EN.2#1|Article 2(1) of Regulation (EU) No 648/2012]] to clear under [[2012R0648_EN.17|Article 17 of Regulation (EU) No 648/2012]] or recognised under [[2012R0648_EN.25|Article 25 of Regulation (EU) No 648/2012]] or authorised under a pre-existing national authorisation regime for a period of less than three years on 2 July 2014 may, before
3 January 2018

, apply to its *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* for permission to avail itself of transitional arrangements. The *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* may decide that this Article does not apply to the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* in respect of *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(24)|transferable securities]]* and money market instruments, for a transitional period until
3 July 2020

.

Where such a transitional period is approved, the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* cannot benefit from the access rights under [[2014R0600_EN.36|Article 36]] or this Article in respect of *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(24)|transferable securities]]* and money market instruments for the duration of that transitional arrangement. The *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(18)|competent authority]]* shall notify members of the college of competent authorities for the *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* and ESMA when a transitional period is approved. ESMA shall publish a list of all notifications that it receives.

Where a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* which has been approved for the transitional arrangements under this paragraph is connected by *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(21)|close links]]* to one or more *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venues]]*, those *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venues]]* shall not benefit from access rights under [[2014R0600_EN.36|Article 36]] or this Article in respect of *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(24)|transferable securities]]* and money market instruments for the duration of the transitional arrangement.

A *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* which is authorised during the three year period prior to entry into force, but is formed by a merger or acquisition involving at least one *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]* authorised prior to that period, shall not be permitted to apply for the transitional arrangements under this paragraph.
## 6.
ESMA shall develop draft regulatory technical standards to specify:
### (a)
the specific conditions under which an access request may be denied by a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]*, including the anticipated volume of transactions, the number and type of users, arrangements for managing operational risk and complexity or other factors creating significant undue risks;
### (b)
the conditions under which access must be permitted by a *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]*, including confidentiality of information provided regarding *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(9)|financial instruments]]* during the development phase, the non-discriminatory and transparent basis as regards clearing fees, collateral requirements and operational requirements regarding margining;
### (c)
the conditions under which granting access will threaten the smooth and orderly functioning of markets or would adversely affect systemic risk;
### (d)
the procedure for making a notification under paragraph 5;
### (e)
the conditions for non-discriminatory treatment in terms of how contracts traded on that *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(16)|trading venue]]* are treated in terms of collateral requirements and netting of economically equivalent contracts and cross-margining with correlated contracts cleared by the same *[[2014R0600_EN.2#(31)|CCP]]*.

ESMA shall submit those draft regulatory technical standards to the Commission by 3 July 2015.

Power is delegated to the Commission to adopt the regulatory technical standards referred to in the first subparagraph in accordance with Articles [[2010R1095_EN.10|10]] to [[2010R1095_EN.14|14]] of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010.
