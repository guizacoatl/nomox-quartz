# Article 25 - Recognition of a third-country CCP

## 1.
A *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* established in a third country may provide *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* services to *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(14)|clearing members]]* or *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(4)|trading venues]]* established in the Union only where that *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* is recognised by ESMA.
## 2.
ESMA, after consulting the authorities referred to in paragraph 3, may recognise a *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* established in a third country that has applied for recognition to provide certain *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* services or activities where:
### (a)
the Commission has adopted an implementing act in accordance with paragraph 6;
### (b)
the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* is authorised in the relevant third country, and is subject to effective supervision and enforcement ensuring full compliance with the prudential requirements applicable in that third country;
### (c)
cooperation arrangements have been established pursuant to paragraph 7;
### (d)
the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* is established or authorised in a third country that is considered as having equivalent systems for anti-money-laundering and combating the financing of terrorism to those of the Union in accordance with the criteria set out in the common understanding between Member States on third-country equivalence under Directive 2005/60/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 26 October 2005 on the prevention of the use of the financial system for the purpose of money laundering and terrorist financing


.
## 3.
When assessing whether the conditions referred to in paragraph 2 are met, ESMA shall consult:
### (a)
the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(13)|competent authority]]* of a Member State in which the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* provides or intends to provide *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* services and which has been selected by the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]*;
### (b)
the competent authorities responsible for the supervision of the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(14)|clearing members]]* of the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* that are established in the three Member States which make or are anticipated by the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* to make the largest contributions to the default fund of the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* referred to in [[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.42|Article 42]] on an aggregate basis over a one-year period;
### (c)
the competent authorities responsible for the supervision of *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(4)|trading venues]]* located in the Union, served or to be served by the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]*;
### (d)
the competent authorities supervising *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCPs]]* established in the Union with which *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(12)|interoperability arrangements]]* have been established;
### (e)
the relevant members of the ESCB of the Member States in which the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* provides or intends to provide *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* services and the relevant members of the ESCB responsible for the oversight of the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCPs]]* with which *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(12)|interoperability arrangements]]* have been established;
### (f)
the central banks of issue of the most relevant Union currencies of the financial instruments cleared or to be cleared.
## 4.
The *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* referred to in paragraph 1 shall submit its application to ESMA.
The applicant *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* shall provide ESMA with all information necessary for its recognition. Within 30 working days of receipt, ESMA shall assess whether the application is complete. If the application is not complete, ESMA shall set a deadline by which the applicant *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* has to provide additional information.

The recognition decision shall be based on the conditions set out in paragraph 2 and shall be independent of any assessment as the basis for the equivalence decision as referred to in [[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.13#3|Article 13(3)]].

ESMA shall consult the authorities and entities referred to in paragraph 3 prior to taking its decision.

Within 180 working days of the submission of a complete application, ESMA shall inform the applicant *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* in writing, with a fully reasoned explanation, whether the recognition has been granted or refused.

ESMA shall publish on its website a list of the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCPs]]* recognised in accordance with this Regulation.
## 5.
ESMA shall, after consulting the authorities and entities referred to in paragraph 3, review the recognition of the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* established in a third country where that *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* has extended the range of its activities and services in the Union. That review shall be conducted in accordance with paragraphs 2, 3 and 4. ESMA may withdraw the recognition of that *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* where the conditions set out in paragraph 2 are no longer met and in the same circumstances as those described in [[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.20|Article 20]].
## 6.
The Commission may adopt an implementing act under [[2011R0182_EN.5|Article 5 of Regulation (EU) No 182/2011]], determining that the legal and supervisory arrangements of a third country ensure that *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCPs]]* authorised in that third country comply with legally binding requirements which are equivalent to the requirements laid down in Title IV of this Regulation, that those *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCPs]]* are subject to effective supervision and enforcement in that third country on an ongoing basis and that the legal framework of that third country provides for an effective equivalent system for the recognition of *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCPs]]* authorised under third-country legal regimes.
## 7.
ESMA shall establish cooperation arrangements with the relevant competent authorities of third countries whose legal and supervisory frameworks have been recognised as equivalent to this Regulation in accordance with paragraph 6. Such arrangements shall specify at least:
### (a)
the mechanism for the exchange of information between ESMA and the competent authorities of the third countries concerned, including access to all information requested by ESMA regarding *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCPs]]* authorised in third countries;
### (b)
the mechanism for prompt notification to ESMA where a third-country *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(13)|competent authority]]* deems a *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* it is supervising to be in breach of the conditions of its authorisation or of other law to which it is subject;
### (c)
the mechanism for prompt notification to ESMA by a third-country *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(13)|competent authority]]* where a *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* it is supervising has been granted the right to provide *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* services to *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(14)|clearing members]]* or *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(15)|clients]]* established in the Union;
### (d)
the procedures concerning the coordination of supervisory activities including, where appropriate, on-site inspections.
## 8.
In order to ensure consistent application of this Article, ESMA shall develop draft regulatory technical standards specifying the information that the applicant *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* shall provide ESMA in its application for recognition.
ESMA shall submit those draft regulatory technical standards to the Commission by 30 September 2012.

Power is delegated to the Commission to adopt the regulatory technical standards referred to in the first subparagraph in accordance with Articles [[2010R1095_EN.10|10]] to [[2010R1095_EN.14|14]] of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010.
