# Article 14 - Authorisation of a CCP

## 1.
Where a legal person established in the Union intends to provide *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* services as a *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]*, it shall apply for authorisation to the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(13)|competent authority]]* of the Member State where it is established (the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]*’s *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(13)|competent authority]]*), in accordance with the procedure set out in [[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.17|Article 17]].
## 2.
Once authorisation has been granted in accordance with [[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.17|Article 17]], it shall be effective for the entire territory of the Union.
## 3.
Authorisation referred to in paragraph 1 shall be granted only for activities linked to *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* and shall specify the services or activities which the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* is authorised to provide or perform including the classes of financial instruments covered by such authorisation.
## 4.
A *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* shall comply at all times with the conditions necessary for authorisation.
A *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* shall, without undue delay, notify the *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(13)|competent authority]]* of any material changes affecting the conditions for authorisation.
## 5.
Authorisation referred to in paragraph 1 shall not prevent Member States from adopting or continuing to apply, in respect of *[[2012R0648EMIR1_EN.2#(1)|CCPs]]* established in their territory, additional requirements including certain requirements for authorisation under Directive 2006/48/EC.
