## 1.
Counterparties shall clear all *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(7)|OTC derivative contracts]]* pertaining to a class of *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(7)|OTC derivatives]]* that has been declared subject to the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* obligation in accordance with [[2012R0648_EN.5#2|Article 5(2)]], if those contracts fulfil both of the following conditions:
### (a)
they have been concluded in one of the following ways:
#### (i)
between two financial counterparties that meet the conditions set out in the second subparagraph of [[2012R0648_EN.4a#1|Article 4a(1)]];
#### (ii)
between a *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(8)|financial counterparty]]* that meets the conditions set out in the second subparagraph of [[2012R0648_EN.4a#1|Article 4a(1)]] and a *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(9)|non-financial counterparty]]* that meets the conditions set out in the second subparagraph of [[2012R0648_EN.10#1|Article 10(1)]];
#### (iii)
between two non-financial counterparties that meet the conditions set out in the second subparagraph of [[2012R0648_EN.10#1|Article 10(1)]];
#### (iv)
between, on the one side, a *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(8)|financial counterparty]]* that meets the conditions set out in the second subparagraph of [[2012R0648_EN.4a#1|Article 4a(1)]] or a *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(9)|non-financial counterparty]]* that meets the conditions set out in the second subparagraph of [[2012R0648_EN.10#1|Article 10(1)]], and, on the other side, an entity established in a third country that would be subject to the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* obligation if it were established in the Union;
#### (v)
between two entities established in one or more third countries that would be subject to the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* obligation if they were established in the Union, provided that the contract has a direct, substantial and foreseeable effect within the Union or where such an obligation is necessary or appropriate to prevent the evasion of any provisions of this Regulation; and
### (b)
they are entered into or novated on or after the date on which the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* obligation takes effect, provided that, on the date they are entered into or novated, both counterparties meet the conditions set out in point (a).

The obligation to clear all *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(7)|OTC derivative contracts]]* shall not apply to contracts concluded in the situations referred to in the first subparagraph, point (a)(iv), between, on the one side, a *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(8)|financial counterparty]]* that meets the conditions set out in [[2012R0648_EN.4a#1|Article 4a(1)]], second subparagraph, or a *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(9)|non-financial counterparty]]* that meets the conditions set out in [[2012R0648_EN.10#1|Article 10(1)]], second subparagraph, and, on the other side, a *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(10)|pension scheme arrangement]]* that is established in a third country and that operates on a national basis, provided that it is authorised, supervised and recognised under national law, and where its primary purpose is to provide retirement benefits and it is exempted from the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* obligation under that national law.
## 2.
Without prejudice to risk-mitigation techniques under [[2012R0648_EN.11|Article 11]], *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(7)|OTC derivative contracts]]* that are intragroup transactions as described in [[2012R0648_EN.3|Article 3]] shall not be subject to the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* obligation.

The exemption set out in the first subparagraph shall apply only:
### (a)
where two counterparties established in the Union belonging to the same *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(16)|group]]* have first notified their respective competent authorities in writing that they intend to make use of the exemption for the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(7)|OTC derivative contracts]]* concluded between each other. The notification shall be made not less than 30 calendar days before the use of the exemption. Within 30 calendar days after receipt of that notification, the competent authorities may object to the use of this exemption if the transactions between the counterparties do not meet the conditions laid down in [[2012R0648_EN.3|Article 3]], without prejudice to the right of the competent authorities to object after that period of 30 calendar days has expired where those conditions are no longer met. If there is disagreement between the competent authorities, ESMA may assist those authorities in reaching an agreement in accordance with its powers under [[2010R1095_EN.19|Article 19 of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010]];
### (b)
to *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(7)|OTC derivative contracts]]* between two counterparties belonging to the same *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(16)|group]]* which are established in a Member State and in a third country, where the counterparty established in the Union has been authorised to apply the exemption by its *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(13)|competent authority]]* within 30 calendar days after it has been notified by the counterparty established in the Union, provided that the conditions laid down in [[2012R0648_EN.3|Article 3]] are met. The *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(13)|competent authority]]* shall notify ESMA of that decision.
## 3.
The *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(7)|OTC derivative contracts]]* that are subject to the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* obligation pursuant to paragraph 1 shall be cleared in a *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(1)|CCP]]* authorised under [[2012R0648_EN.14|Article 14]] or recognised under [[2012R0648_EN.25|Article 25]] to clear that class of *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(7)|OTC derivatives]]* and listed in the register in accordance with [[2012R0648_EN.6#2#b|Article 6(2)(b)]].

For that purpose a counterparty shall become a *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(14)|clearing member]]*, a *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(15)|client]]*, or shall establish indirect *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* arrangements with a *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(14)|clearing member]]*, provided that those arrangements do not increase counterparty risk and ensure that the assets and positions of the counterparty benefit from protection with equivalent effect to that referred to in Articles [[2012R0648_EN.39|39]] and [[2012R0648_EN.48|48]].
## 3a.
Without being obliged to contract, *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(14)|clearing members]]* and *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(15)|clients]]* which provide *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* services, whether directly or indirectly, shall provide those services under fair, reasonable, non-discriminatory and transparent commercial terms. Such *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(14)|clearing members]]* and *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(15)|clients]]* shall take all reasonable measures to identify, prevent, manage and monitor conflicts of interest, in particular between the trading unit and the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* unit, that may adversely affect the fair, reasonable, non-discriminatory and transparent provision of *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* services. Such measures shall also be taken where trading and *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* services are provided by different legal entities belonging to the same *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(16)|group]]*.

*[[2012R0648_EN.2#(14)|Clearing members]]* and *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(15)|clients]]* shall be permitted to *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(23)|control]]* the risks related to the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* services offered.

The Commission is empowered to adopt delegated acts in accordance with [[2012R0648_EN.82|Article 82]] to supplement this Regulation by specifying the conditions under which the commercial terms referred to in the first subparagraph of this paragraph are to be considered to be fair, reasonable, non-discriminatory and transparent, based on the following:
### (a)
fairness and transparency requirements with respect to fees, prices, discount policies and other general contractual terms and conditions regarding the price list, without prejudice to the confidentiality of contractual arrangements with individual counterparties;
### (b)
factors that constitute reasonable commercial terms to ensure unbiased and rational contractual arrangements;
### (c)
requirements that facilitate *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* services on a fair and non-discriminatory basis, having regard to related costs and risks, so that any differences in prices charged are proportionate to costs, risks and benefits; and
### (d)
risk *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(23)|control]]* criteria for the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(14)|clearing member]]* or *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(15)|client]]* related to the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(3)|clearing]]* services offered.
## 4.
In order to ensure consistent application of this Article, ESMA shall develop draft regulatory technical standards specifying the contracts that are considered to have a direct, substantial and foreseeable effect within the Union or the cases where it is necessary or appropriate to prevent the evasion of any provision of this Regulation as referred to in paragraph 1(a)(v), and the types of indirect contractual arrangements that meet the conditions referred to in the second subparagraph of paragraph 3.

ESMA shall submit those draft regulatory technical standards to the Commission by 30 September 2012.

Power is delegated to the Commission to adopt the regulatory technical standards referred to in the first subparagraph in accordance with Articles [[2010R1095_EN.10|10]] to [[2010R1095_EN.14|14]] of Regulation (EU) No 1095/2010.
## 5.
Paragraph 1 of this Article shall not apply with respect to *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(7)|OTC derivative contracts]]* that are concluded by *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(30)|covered bond]]* entities in connection with a *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(30)|covered bond]]*, or by a securitisation special purpose entity in connection with a securitisation, within the meaning of Regulation (EU) 2017/2402 of the European Parliament and of the Council provided that:
### (a)
in the case of securitisation special purpose entities, the securitisation special purpose entity shall solely issue securitisations that meet the requirements of [[2012R0648_EN.18|Article 18]], and of Articles [[2012R0648_EN.19|19]] to 22 or 23 to 26 of Regulation (EU) 2017/2402 (the Securitisation Regulation);
### (b)
the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(7)|OTC derivative contract]]* is used only to hedge interest rate or currency mismatches under the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(30)|covered bond]]* or securitisation; and
### (c)
the arrangements under the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(30)|covered bond]]* or securitisation adequately mitigate *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(11)|counterparty credit risk]]* with respect to the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(7)|OTC derivative contracts]]* concluded by the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(31)|covered bond entity]]* or securitisation special purpose entity in connection with the *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(30)|covered bond]]* or securitisation.
## 6.
In order to ensure consistent application of this Article, and taking into account the need to prevent regulatory arbitrage, the ESAs shall develop draft regulatory technical standards specifying criteria for establishing which arrangements under *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(30)|covered bonds]]* or securitisations adequately mitigate *[[2012R0648_EN.2#(11)|counterparty credit risk]]*, within the meaning of paragraph 5.

The ESAs shall submit those draft regulatory technical standards to the Commission by 18 July 2018.

Power is delegated to the Commission to supplement this Regulation by adopting the regulatory technical standards referred to in this paragraph in accordance with Articles [[2012R0648_EN.10|10]] to 14 of Regulations (EU) No 1093/2010, (EU) No 1094/2010 or (EU) No 1095/2010.
