# ESMA_QA_2221

> **Status:** ✅ Answer Published

---

## Regulatory Context

**Level 1 Regulation:** MiCA

**Topic:** Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP)

**Subject Matter:** Entities who have not applied for, or whose application for authorisation as CASPs has been refused by the end of the transition period


---

## Question

**Submission Date:** 21-06-2024

What happens to an entity providing crypto-asset services in accordance with
applicable law before 30 December 2024 that has not applied for authorisation as a
 end of the transition period?

---

## ESMA Answer

**Answer Date:** 04-07-2024

Where an entity providing crypto-asset services in accordance with applicable law
before 30 December 2024 has not been authorised as a CASP by the end of the
transition period applicable in the relevant Member State, it must cease providing
crypto-asset services. Where such entities do not seek a MiCA authorisation, they
should consider at an early stage how they will wind down their operations in a
manner that avoids negative impact on their clients in accordance, if relevant, with
applicable laws.
